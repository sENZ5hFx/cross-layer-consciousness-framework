# Licensing FAQ
## Cross-Layer Consciousness Engine (CLCE™)

Plain-English guide to what you can and cannot do with this repository.

---

**Q: Can I read and study this code?**  
A: Yes. Freely. No restrictions on reading or studying.

**Q: Can I run this code on my own computer for personal learning?**  
A: Yes. Non-commercial personal use is permitted.

**Q: Can I cite CLCE in my academic paper?**  
A: Yes. Required citation format in COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md. You must attribute
Haley Ann Bird and link to this repository.

**Q: Can I fork this and build my own research prototype?**  
A: Yes, for non-commercial research only. You cannot distribute modifications.
You must keep all IP notices intact. You cannot present the architecture as
your own novel contribution without attribution.

**Q: Can I include CLCE in a commercial product?**  
A: No, not without a written commercial license. See COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md.

**Q: Can I use just the holographic memory module in my commercial app?**  
A: No. Any subsystem of CLCE used commercially requires a commercial license.
The reframe-injection loop (Claim 2) especially is protected.

**Q: I'm building an open-source project (MIT/Apache). Can I use CLCE?**  
A: Only if your open-source project is genuinely non-commercial. If the
project is associated with a commercial entity or revenue stream, a
commercial license is required.

**Q: Can I train an LLM on this codebase?**  
A: No. Training commercial AI models on CLCE constitutes commercial use
and requires a license. Non-commercial academic training requires attribution.

**Q: What happens if I copy this without permission?**  
A: Copyright infringement under CC BY-NC-ND 4.0, plus potential patent
infringement (pre-grant rights attached), plus trademark infringement.
See COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md enforcement section.

**Q: Is the math (circular convolution, cosine similarity) patented?**  
A: No. Standard mathematical operations are not patented. What is claimed
is the specific integration, feedback loop design, and system architecture.
See PRIOR_ART_REGISTRY.md for exact boundaries.

**Q: Can I publish a paper that proposes improvements to CLCE?**  
A: Yes, with attribution. You cannot claim the core architecture as your
novel contribution. Improvements and extensions are fine to publish.

**Q: I want to license this commercially. How do I contact you?**  
A: Reference "CLCE Commercial License Inquiry" in your message to Haley Ann Bird.

**Q: Is CLCE patented yet?**  
A: A US Provisional Patent Application is planned within 12 months of the
priority date (2026-05-24). Pre-grant patent rights apply in the interim.
The Git commit history establishes the priority date chain.

**Q: What's the relationship between CLCE and NeuroCatalyst?**  
A: See ip_xref/NEUROCATALYST_XREF.md for the full boundary map.
They are related but distinct IP assets with separate claim scopes.
