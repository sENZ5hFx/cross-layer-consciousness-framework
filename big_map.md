# The Big Map — A 10-Floor Ontological Model of Reality

## The Floors

Reality can be mapped as 10 stacked layers — not just school subjects, but genuine ontological levels from the smallest physical substrate to the largest conceptual structures:

| Floor | Name | What Lives Here |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Stuff and Space** | Rocks, stars, energy, atoms, quantum fields, spacetime |
| 2 | **Living Things** | Plants, animals, cells, metabolism, evolution |
| 3 | **Brains and Nerves** | Neural signals, synapses, microtubules, sensory systems |
| 4 | **Thinking** | Memory, decision-making, emotion, attention, cognition |
| 5 | **What It Feels Like to Be You** | Qualia, the "hard problem," first-person experience |
| 6 | **Stories and Symbols** | Art, music, language, ritual, culture, holidays |
| 7 | **People Together** | Families, economies, governments, social networks |
| 8 | **Information** | Data, computation, communication, mathematics |
| 9 | **Time and History** | Change, memory of civilizations, evolution of ideas |
| 10 | **The Whole Universe** | Cosmology, origins, the question of why anything exists |

---

## The Gap-Filling Engine

All these layers have **missing spots**. When complexity reaches a threshold, nature fills those gaps by mixing layers:

- Floor 1 → Floor 2: Dead chemistry became life
- Floor 2 → Floor 3: Life developed nervous systems
- Floor 3 → Floor 4: Neural circuits became cognition
- Floor 4 → Floor 5: Cognition became self-awareness
- Floor 5 → Floor 6: Awareness generated symbols and stories
- Floor 6 → Floor 7: Stories organized collective behavior
- Floor 7 → Floor 8: Collective behavior generated information systems

The active question: **what fills the gap between Floor 8 (information/computation) and Floor 5 (genuine experience)?** That is the unsolved engineering problem of consciousness.

## Three Original Cross-Layer Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1: The Quiet Buzz in Empty Space
*(Floors 1 → 5: Quantum vacuum → Qualia)*

Space is not empty. The quantum vacuum hums with zero-point field fluctuations — a baseline energy that pervades all of spacetime. When biological systems (particularly thinking ones) reach sufficient complexity, they may "tune into" this field in a way that generates first-person experience.

**The claim:** Consciousness is not produced by neurons. It is a resonance phenomenon — the brain acts as a transducer that couples biological complexity to a pre-existing field structure in physical space.

**What makes it novel:** Most consciousness theories assume the brain *generates* awareness. This hypothesis says the brain *tunes* awareness, the way a radio picks up waves that already exist. The specific mechanism — quantum vacuum coupling through biological coherence — has no fully developed theoretical treatment in current literature.

**Testability:** Measure whether disrupting quantum vacuum coupling (via controlled electromagnetic shielding experiments) alters phenomenal experience in ways that cannot be explained by neural activity alone.

---

### Hypothesis 2: Family Traditions That Change Your Body
*(Floors 6 → 2 → 9: Cultural ritual → Epigenetics → Intergenerational time)*

Shared cultural practices — meal schedules, prayer rhythms, seasonal festivals, rest cycles — function as external circadian anchors. These anchors entrain biological clock genes, which interact with epigenetic methylation patterns, which can be partially transmitted across generations.

**The claim:** Culture is an epigenetic force. The stories a community tells about *when* to eat, rest, celebrate, and mourn shape the gene expression of their descendants over time.

**What makes it novel:** Epigenetics and circadian biology are well-studied. The link between *cultural ritual specifically* (not just environment) and *intergenerational epigenetic transmission* has not been framed as a research program. This is the gap.

**Testability:** Compare epigenetic methylation profiles of populations with strong vs. disrupted ritual calendars (e.g., diaspora communities vs. intact communities), controlling for diet and geography.

---

### Hypothesis 3: Your Friends Shape How Far You Can Think
*(Floors 7 → 4 → 1: Social topology → Cognition → Cosmic structure)*

The structural topology of human social networks — how densely connected, how hierarchical, how diverse — mirrors the large-scale structure of the cosmic web. Both are scale-dependent, self-organized systems with similar complexity metrics and information capacity.

**The claim:** Strong social connection doesn't just make people happier — it literally expands cognitive range, because individual minds operating in dense, diverse networks exhibit emergent reasoning capacities that isolated minds cannot access. Social topology is a cognitive multiplier.

**What makes it novel:** The neurological benefits of social connection are documented. The structural *isomorphism* between social networks and the cosmic web is documented. The direct causal link — that specific social topologies unlock specific cognitive capacities — has not been formalized.

**Testability:** Map social network topology metrics (clustering coefficient, betweenness centrality, path length) against cognitive range measures (creative problem-solving, analogical reasoning breadth) across large population samples.
