# Empirical Testability Protocols

This document defines the real-world experimental protocols for testing the three original cross-layer hypotheses from the Big Map. These are not software benchmarks — they are proposals for actual scientific studies.

---

## Hypothesis 1: The Quiet Buzz in Empty Space
*(Floors 1 → 5: Quantum vacuum coupling → Qualia)*

### What Would Count as Evidence

**Supporting evidence:**
- Phenomenal experience is measurably altered by conditions that affect quantum vacuum coupling (e.g., extreme magnetic shielding, Casimir effect proximity) in ways not fully explained by standard neural activity
- EEG/MEG patterns during altered states (deep meditation, psychedelic states, anesthesia emergence) show anomalous coherence signatures that correlate with quantum vacuum field parameters
- Cross-species consciousness proxies (behavioral markers of awareness) scale with biological systems' known capacity for quantum coherence, not just neural complexity alone

**Study design:**
1. Faraday cage + mu-metal shielding experiments: compare phenomenal report (via structured subjective report protocols) and neural activity (EEG) under standard conditions vs. maximally shielded conditions
2. Casimir force proximity study: non-invasive measurement of whether cellular systems in high Casimir-effect environments show different coherence signatures
3. Meta-analysis of anesthesia studies: test whether quantum coherence disruption (rather than neurotransmitter blockade alone) better predicts consciousness loss threshold

**Falsification:** If no measurable difference in subjective report or neural coherence patterns is found under any vacuum-coupling manipulation, and this holds across multiple independent studies, the hypothesis is falsified.

## Hypothesis 2: Family Traditions That Change Your Body
*(Floors 6 → 2 → 9: Cultural ritual → Epigenetics → Intergenerational transmission)*

### What Would Count as Evidence

**Supporting evidence:**
- Populations with intact, regular ritual calendars show systematically different epigenetic methylation profiles at circadian clock genes (CLOCK, BMAL1, PER1/2/3, CRY1/2) compared to populations with disrupted or absent ritual calendars
- The methylation differences persist after controlling for diet, geography, socioeconomic status, and direct environmental exposure
- The differences are partially present in second-generation diaspora members who no longer practice the rituals themselves (suggesting intergenerational transmission, not just direct entrainment)

**Study design:**
1. **Phase 1 — Cross-population methylation survey:** Recruit 4 population groups: (a) intact ritual calendar community, (b) same ethnic group, diaspora, rituals maintained, (c) same ethnic group, diaspora, rituals abandoned, (d) unrelated control population matched for latitude (circadian anchor). Collect blood samples. Measure methylation at circadian clock gene promoters via RRBS (Reduced Representation Bisulfite Sequencing).
2. **Phase 2 — Longitudinal intervention:** Follow diaspora group (c) through a voluntary ritual re-adoption program for 24 months. Measure methylation change at 6-month intervals.
3. **Phase 3 — Second-generation study:** Recruit adult children of Phase 1 group (c) parents. Test whether parental ritual abandonment is associated with offspring methylation profiles, controlling for offspring's own ritual participation.

**Falsification:** If Phase 1 shows no significant methylation differences between groups after controlling for diet and latitude, the cultural-epigenetic link hypothesis is falsified at the population level.

## Hypothesis 3: Your Friends Shape How Far You Can Think
*(Floors 7 → 4 → 1: Social topology → Cognition → Cosmic structure)*

### What Would Count as Evidence

**Supporting evidence:**
- Specific social network topology metrics (clustering coefficient, betweenness centrality, structural holes) predict cognitive range measures (analogical breadth, remote associate task performance, creative problem-solving) above and beyond IQ, education, and social support quality
- The effect is directional and causal: changes in network topology (natural experiments: moving to a new city, joining a diverse organization, losing key network connections) precede measurable changes in cognitive range
- The topology-cognition relationship mirrors the topology-complexity relationship observed in the cosmic web (same scaling laws, same relationship between local density and long-range connectivity)

**Study design:**
1. **Phase 1 — Cross-sectional survey:** N=5,000. Collect: ego network topology (via standard network analysis survey), cognitive range battery (RAT, AUT, analogical reasoning, divergent thinking), controls (IQ proxy, education, income, social support quality, loneliness scale).
2. **Phase 2 — Natural experiment longitudinal:** Follow N=500 participants through major network disruption events (relocation, job change, bereavement). Measure topology and cognitive range at 0, 6, 12, 24 months.
3. **Phase 3 — Topology mapping:** For a subset of N=100, map full network topology in detail and test whether topology metrics match cosmic web scaling parameters (fractal dimension, clustering coefficient distribution, information capacity estimates).

**Falsification:** If network topology metrics add no significant predictive power over standard social support measures for cognitive range outcomes (after controlling for confounds), the specific topology-cognition hypothesis is falsified, even if general social connection effects remain.

## Cross-Hypothesis Integration Test

If all three hypotheses hold individually, a fourth study becomes possible:

**The Triple Integration Study:** Communities with strong ritual calendars (H2) embedded in dense, topologically rich social networks (H3) should show the highest rates of reported phenomenal richness and the most distinctive neural coherence signatures (H1). This would suggest the three floor-crossing mechanisms are not independent but mutually amplifying — which is precisely the prediction of the gap-filling engine.
