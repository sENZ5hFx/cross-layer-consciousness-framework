# Consciousness Models: A Cross-Framework Comparison

All six models below challenge the standard computational view of the brain — the idea that mind is simply what a sufficiently complex information-processing system *does*. Each proposes a different answer to the same question: **if consciousness is fundamental, what is the antenna that picks it up?**

## The Philosophical Fault Line

All six models sit on the same side of a 400-year-old debate — against **reductive materialism** (mind is just complicated matter) and against **Cartesian dualism** (mind and matter are separate substances). Instead, each proposes a third option:

- **IIT and GWT**: Functionalist — mind is a *pattern* that can exist in any substrate
- **Orch-OR and HBRF**: Biological substrate — mind requires specific physical hardware
- **UCF and QTTC**: Idealist / field-based — consciousness is prior to matter

The 2025 adversarial collaboration published in *Nature* directly tested IIT vs. GWT in human brains and found both were only *partially* right — prefrontal ignition (GWT signature) was weaker than predicted, and sustained synchronization (IIT prediction) was not required. No single model currently accounts for all the data.
